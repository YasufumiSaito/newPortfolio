My Story

I like climbing. The reason is simple. No matter how hard it is, and even if the steps are slow, the effort you made there never betray you.

When the New York Times published "Snow Fall" in December, 2012, I was a reporter for a Japanese local daily.

I was thrilled and eager to learn how to do it. But given the distance between the NYT and where I stood,

I was overwhelmed, and it seemed too far.

But one thing for sure was something important for journalism already happened.

Let's climb the mountain, I decided, starting from studying English at the very beginning

Now you see this wasn't easy choice for me. No, it really wasn't.

It took 2 years, including stay in Melbourne, to enroll a journalism school to earn new skills.

When I got to Medill School of Journalism, I thought I could finally step in the new world.

But it was just a beginning.

Even teachers

In January 2016, I started coding. HTML5, CSS3. They are awesome

Looking back to the past, I sometimes think

We journalists can be wizards, and stories can be more immersive, interactive and entertaining as people want.

Revolutionize

Standing on a sweet spot where journalism and web technology intersect, I want to be a part of its future.
